Simha is a 2010 Telugu action-drama film about a righteous college professor, Srimannarayana, who is provoked to unleash his violent past when he discovers a dangerous plot involving his love interest Janaki and a gangster who is the son of a man he accidentally woke from a 28-year coma. The film stars Nandamuri Balakrishna and Nayanthara and was directed by Boyapati Srinu.Protagonist: Srimannarayana is a beloved but feared college professor who despises injustice and takes matters into his own hands. Plot: His love interest, Janaki, reveals that her father is in a business feud with Veerakesavudu's son, Gopi. To end the rivalry, Veerakesavudu orders Janaki to marry Gopi. Conflict: Srimannarayana must now confront his past to protect Janaki and thwart the dangerous plan, leading to a violent showdown. Genre: The film is an action-drama with themes of justice and family.

Nandini is engaged to another man, and the story follows their journey as their friendship blossoms into love, leading to comical and dramatic situations surrounding the upcoming marriage. The narrative centers on the complexities of their forbidden love, the interference of comical characters, and the eventual resolution where Murthy gives his blessings for Venky and Nandini to marry.
